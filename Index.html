Brief Biography

Hernán B. Rodríguez graduated in Biochemistry in 2001 at the National University of Patagonia San Juan Bosco in Comodoro Rivadavia. He obtained his PhD in Chemical Sciences at the University of Buenos Aires (UBA) in 2009. He belongs to the staff of the National Scientific and Technological Research Council of Argentina (CONICET), actually working at the Institute of Physical Chemistry of Materials, Environment and Energy (INQUIMAE) in Buenos Aires. Present research interests are the development of heterogeneous photosensitizers, the photophysics and photochemistry of dyes in nanostructured heterogeneous media and the study of molecular interactions and energy and charge transfer processes.

Expertise: Transmission and reflectance absorption spectroscopy; steady-state and time-resolved emission spectroscopy; laser flash photolysis and transient absorption spectroscopy in both transmittance and diffuse reflectance modes; and laser-induced optoacoustic spectroscopy. Experienced in modeling and investigating energy and charge transfer processes, particularly in highly concentrated heterogeneous media. Currently focused on the development of photosensitizing and bioimaging systems with potential applications in biological and environmental contexts.
