Research Interests

My scientific interests focus on nanoparticulate and nanostructured photosensitizing systems, as well as the photophysics and photochemistry of dyes in heterogeneous media, including inert and active (semiconducting) microparticles, nanoparticles, and thin films. I am particularly interested in molecular interactions and energy- and charge-transfer processes.

My research lies within the field of Physical Chemistry, with an emphasis on materials science, photophysics, and photochemistry. Through my work, I have gained extensive experience in the use and interpretation of a wide range of spectroscopic techniques, including transmission and diffuse reflectance absorption spectroscopy; steady-state and time-resolved fluorescence and phosphorescence; fluorescence anisotropy; and the study of transient species using transient absorption spectroscopies, such as laser flash photolysis (by transmission and/or diffuse reflectance). I also have experience with laser-induced optoacoustic spectroscopy, X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS), Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR), and kinetic characterization methods (e.g., modulation techniques and stopped-flow methods), as well as general materials characterization techniques (e.g., TEM, SEM, DLS, TGA, XRD, etc.).

In addition, I have developed novel experimental methodologies and physico-mathematical models that leverage spectroscopic data to investigate physicochemical processes.

Currently focused on the development of photosensitizing and bioimaging systems with potential applications in biological and environmental contexts.
